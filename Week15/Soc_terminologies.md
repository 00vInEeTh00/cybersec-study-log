## 1. Core SOC Concepts & Monitoring Terms

* **Security Operations Center (SOC):** A centralized cybersecurity function, team, or facility responsible for continuous monitoring, detection, analysis, investigation, and response to security threats across an organization’s IT infrastructure. It integrates people, processes, and technologies to protect confidentiality, integrity, and availability.
* **CSOC (Cyber Security Operations Center):** An alternative term for SOC emphasizing cybersecurity-focused operations.
* **Monitoring:** Continuous observation of systems, networks, endpoints, applications, and user activities to detect abnormal or malicious behavior.
* **Security Event:** Any observable occurrence within a system or network (e.g., login attempt, file access, configuration change). Events are not necessarily malicious.
* **Security Incident:** A confirmed event that violates security policies or threatens systems, data, or operations and requires formal incident response.
* **Alert:** A notification generated by security tools when predefined conditions or suspicious activities are detected and require analyst review.
* **Log:** A timestamped record of system, application, or network activity used for monitoring, investigation, compliance, and forensics.
* **Log Management:** The process of collecting, storing, normalizing, and analyzing logs to detect anomalies and support investigations.
* **Audit Log:** Logs maintained specifically for compliance verification, audits, and regulatory review.

## 2. SOC Roles, Teams & Organizational Structure

### Analyst Tiers
| Role | Title | Primary Responsibilities |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Tier 1 (L1)** | Triage Analyst | First line of defense; monitor alerts/dashboards; perform initial investigation; validate alerts; escalate confirmed threats. |
| **Tier 2 (L2)** | Incident Responder | Deep investigation and analysis; incident validation; execute containment and response actions; threat analysis. |
| **Tier 3 (L3)** | Threat Hunter / Expert | Advanced threat hunting; malware analysis/reverse engineering; detection engineering; improve SOC detection capability. |

### Security Teams
* **Blue Team:** Defensive security team responsible for monitoring and protecting systems (SOC core function).
* **Red Team:** Simulates real attackers to test defenses and identify weaknesses.
* **Purple Team:** Collaboration between Red and Blue teams to improve detection and response effectiveness.

### Managed Services (MDR) Terms
* **MDR (Managed Detection and Response):** Outsourced SOC service providing 24/7 monitoring, detection, and response.
* **MDR SOC Pod:** Dedicated analyst team assigned to a specific customer environment.
* **Cybersecurity Advisor (CA):** Strategic advisor helping customers improve security posture.

## 3. Detection & Analysis Terminology

* **SIEM (Security Information and Event Management):** Central platform that collects logs, correlates events, detects threats, generates alerts, and supports investigations. (e.g., Splunk, Microsoft Sentinel, IBM QRadar).
* **Correlation Rule:** Predefined logic combining multiple events to detect suspicious patterns.
* **Use Case:** A defined detection scenario describing attack behavior that should trigger alerts.
* **Behavioral Analysis:** Detection method identifying abnormal behavior rather than known signatures.
* **Threat Intelligence (CTI):** Actionable information about threats, attackers, and vulnerabilities gathered from internal or external sources.
* **TIP (Threat Intelligence Platform):** System that aggregates and analyzes threat intelligence feeds.
* **Indicators of Compromise (IOC):** Evidence suggesting compromise (e.g., malicious IPs, file hashes, domains).
* **UEBA/UBA (User and Entity Behavior Analytics):** Technology using analytics and ML to detect abnormal user or system behavior.

### Detection Accuracy
* **True Positive:** Real threat correctly detected.
* **False Positive:** Benign activity flagged as malicious.
* **True Negative:** Legitimate activity correctly ignored.
* **False Negative:** Real attack missed by detection systems.

## 4. Core Security Technologies & Tools

* **EDR (Endpoint Detection and Response):** Monitors endpoint devices and enables detection, investigation, and response actions like isolation.
* **XDR (Extended Detection and Response):** Unified detection across endpoints, networks, email, and cloud.
* **NDR (Network Detection and Response):** Analyzes network traffic behavior to detect threats missed by traditional tools.
* **SOAR (Security Orchestration, Automation, and Response):** Automates IR workflows and integrates tools using playbooks (e.g., Cortex XSOAR).
* **IDS/IPS:** IDS detects suspicious traffic; IPS detects and blocks it.
* **Firewall:** Network security control that filters traffic based on policies.
* **DLP (Data Loss Prevention):** Prevents unauthorized transmission of sensitive data.
* **WAF (Web Application Firewall):** Protects web apps from attacks like SQL injection and XSS.
* **Ticketing System:** Case management system used to track incidents and progress (e.g., ServiceNow).

## 5. SOC Processes & Incident Response

* **Incident Response (IR):** Structured process to identify, contain, eradicate, and recover from incidents.
* **Alert Triage:** Initial evaluation and prioritization of alerts.
* **Incident Analysis:** Examining a confirmed incident to determine cause, scope, and impact.
* **Investigation:** Deep analysis to determine root cause and attacker activity.
* **Escalation:** Transferring incidents to higher-level analysts due to complexity.

### Response Actions
* **Containment:** Limiting damage and preventing spread (e.g., isolating devices).
* **Eradication:** Removing root cause (malware, persistence mechanisms).
* **Recovery:** Restoring systems safely to normal operations.
* **Resolution:** Final closure after threat elimination.
* **Post-Incident Review (Lessons Learned):** Analysis to improve future defenses.

### Guidance Documents
* **Playbook:** Step-by-step instructions for responding to specific incidents.
* **Runbook:** Broader operational procedures for routine SOC tasks.
* **Alert Disposition:** Final classification (Malicious, Benign, or False Positive).

## 6. Threat Hunting & Vulnerability Concepts

* **Threat Hunting:** Proactive search for hidden threats assuming compromise may already exist.
* **Vulnerability:** Weakness in software, hardware, or configuration exploitable by attackers.
* **Vulnerability Management:** Process of identifying, prioritizing, and remediating vulnerabilities.
* **Security Posture Assessment:** Evaluation of an organization’s overall security readiness.

---

## 7. Threat & Attack Terminology

* **Malware:** Malicious software designed to damage or gain unauthorized access.
* **Phishing:** Social engineering via fraudulent communication to steal credentials.
* **Ransomware:** Malware encrypting data and demanding payment for recovery.
* **Brute Force Attack:** Repeated login attempts to guess credentials.
* **Lateral Movement:** Attacker moving across systems after initial compromise.
* **Privilege Escalation:** Gaining unauthorized higher-level permissions.
* **Exfiltration:** Unauthorized transfer of data outside the organization.

---

## 8. Security Frameworks & Models



* **MITRE ATT&CK Framework:** Knowledge base mapping attacker tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs).
* **Cyber Kill Chain:** Attack lifecycle model (Reconnaissance to Actions on Objectives).
* **NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF):** Five functions: Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover.
* **Zero Trust:** Security model based on “never trust, always verify.”
* **Defense in Depth:** Multiple layered security controls protecting systems.

---

## 9. Metrics & Performance Terms

* **MTTD (Mean Time to Detect):** Average time required to discover a threat.
* **MTTR (Mean Time to Respond / Resolve):** Average time taken to contain and fully resolve incidents.
* **Severity Level:** Risk rating assigned based on impact and urgency.

---

## 10. Compliance, Governance & Forensics

* **Compliance:** Adherence to regulatory and security standards.
* **Forensics:** Collection and analysis of digital evidence following incidents.
